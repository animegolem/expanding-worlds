1The first-run guide, all seven pages (EPIC-019) — plain and warm, skippable at every step, ends inside the seeded example
1aPage 1 — what this is, in one breath
A board for your pictures
Drop art on it. Arrange it the way you already do. That's the whole app — until, without any extra ceremony, it quietly becomes a world you can search, map, and read.
skipnext ›
1bPage 2 — the one lesson that must land
Your pictures are safe
Everything you drop here is COPIED. Your own folders and originals are never touched, moved, or changed — and one picture can live in many places at once without existing twice.
skipnext ›
1cPage 3 — hands on the board
No knobs on your art
Inside moves it. Edges stretch it. Corners scale it; just outside a corner turns it. The cursor shows you — and every button in the app names itself and its key when you hover.
skipnext ›
1dPage 4 — words, and the wiki that happens
Any picture can carry words
Its note opens beside it like a page in a book. Write [[a name]] and it's linked — that's the whole wiki. Nothing saves until you actually type something.
skipnext ›
1ePage 5 — doors
Pictures open into boards
Give any picture a board of its own — a map opens into its region, a character into their story. Boards nest as deep as the world goes, and the path at the top is always the way back.
skipnext ›
1fPage 6 — finding it again
Nothing gets lost
Tag things as you arrange them. Search finds every word you've written and every picture you've named. The gallery holds your whole collection — and deleting only ever moves things to a trash that keeps them whole.

1hThe rules of the arc — content-design notes for build
· one idea per page, ≤3 sentences, warm and plain — no feature names, no "node"
· page 2 (copies, never your files) is the load-bearing page; it may not be cut in any shortening
· skip is on every page and never nags; the guide never shows again (a settings action can replay it)
· page 7's pick is optional and only filters which workflow ideas show; a pick could someday seed a real starter world (deferred, letter)
· "start" lands INSIDE the seeded example — the walkthrough teaches claims, the example proves them with real furniture
· pages are the taped-paper voice: Maple, dots for progress, board visible behind
