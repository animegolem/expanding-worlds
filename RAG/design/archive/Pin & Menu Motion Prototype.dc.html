1 The bookmark beat — the signature pin bridges chrome into the canvas

The path-tail bookmark is the app's one signature: the only colored element in the mono chrome, wearing the canonical pin (silhouette iv). Click it — it wiggles like a dog readying its hop, pops up, and pushes DOWN into the canvas with a baby touch of squash-and-stretch; the bookmarks menu sweeps in underneath, anchored to its opener. Esc or click the board to close. No chrome pill — ⌂, the board name, and the pin sit bare on the board at cap-height; hovering the top edge smokes in the low-opacity title strip (the drag handle), and the whole beat stays inside that band. The Tweaks panel switches the menu grammar.

wiggle ~220ms (±8° about the tip, anticipation)

press ~100ms — dips 3px, reseats at its EXACT spot (no drift) · squash scaleY .93
settle ~230ms, one gentle overshoot, no bounce loop
the WHOLE beat stays inside the title-strip band (the would-be chrome)
menu +190ms directional sweep, opacity-led, from the anchor
unpin on close: plain 120ms fade — the ceremony is for arrival
The doctrine note this prototypes: chrome animates opacity only — EXCEPT the pin, which is the one isomorphic object bridging the two materials. It performs a desk beat because it literally enters the canvas. Every other menu in the app gets only the directional sweep below.
