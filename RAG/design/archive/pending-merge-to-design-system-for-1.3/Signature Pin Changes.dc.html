The signature pin pass

Seven decisions, one thread: the red pin becomes the app's signature — on the icon, at the path tail, and as the one object allowed to cross from chrome into the world.

01
The shell eats the window. Frameless on every platform; the board paints edge-to-edge; traffic lights drawn into the board. The hover-revealed title strip — a smoky near-black gradient, not a bar — IS the drag handle; a held chord moves/resizes from anywhere (macOS binding open). → RFC §8 · letter amendment #6
02
App icon crowned. The world-photo reference card, cross-angled, pinned by the red pin — what the app IS, in one tile. → App Icon Document 5a · design-system/assets/icons/app-icon.svg
03
The canonical pin: silhouette iv. Sweeping concave sides, soft rounded tip, carved-red meridian head. Replaces the flat 50/50/50/0 teardrop wherever pins mean places. → design-system/assets/icons/pin-canonical.svg
04
The signature spot. The path-tail bookmark is the app's only bookmark control — so the pin lives there at cap-height beside the board name, as the ONE colored element in the mono chrome, and the one sanctioned chrome→world crossover. → letter amendment #7 · guidelines/pin-signature.html
05
The bookmark beat. Anticipation wiggle (±8° about the tip) → 10px hop with a whisper of stretch → press, reseating at its exact spot — ~700ms, one-shot, entirely inside the strip band. Unpin on close is a plain fade; the ceremony is for arrival. → Pin & Menu Motion Prototype
06
Menu grammar ratified: the cascade. Every menu and popover opens the same way — rows fade in staggered ~30ms, top to bottom, opacity only, ≤190ms ease-out, anchored to its opener. Chrome still animates exactly one property. → prototype §2c · MenuPopover to adopt
07
Every bookmark is a world. Menu rows wear small globes in place of dots — the pin pins down into a globe, echoing the icon. the fordHomeworld
